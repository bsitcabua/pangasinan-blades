Our Story

Forged by Tradition.
Built with Purpose.

Our story began in the 1980s, when bladesmithing was a way of life in Pozorrubio, Pangasinan. At just ten years old, our founder learned the craft by working alongside local blacksmiths, developing the skills and passion that would shape his future.

After years of working overseas and pursuing other ventures, he returned to his roots. In 2008, together with his brother, he established a small workshop that evolved from handcrafted souvenirs into traditional Filipino bolos, tactical knives, and swords.

Today, we proudly continue Pozorrubio's bladesmithing heritage. Our blades are crafted using a combination of precision machinery, skilled craftsmanship, and careful hand-finishing.

The Craft

The Art of Forging

From the first specification to the final fit, each Pangasinan blade is shaped through a hands-on process built around purpose, balance, and dependable performance.

01 01

Plan the Build

Every made-to-order blade begins with its intended use and preferred specifications. Blade form, length, steel, handle, scabbard, finish, and optional engraving are confirmed before work begins.

02 02

Heat the Steel

The selected steel, commonly 5160 carbon steel, is heated in the forge until it reaches a workable condition. The craftsman watches the color and heat closely before shaping begins.

03 03

Forge the Profile

Controlled hammer work establishes the blade's traditional profile, curve, point, and balance. The form is developed gradually to suit the selected design, from utility bolos to longer combat and heritage blades.

04 04

Heat Treat & Quench

After shaping, the blade is heated and quenched to develop the hardness required for its steel. For the catalog's standard 5160 carbon-steel builds, the target specification is 57-60 HRC.

05 05

Grind & Finish

The profile, bevels, and edge geometry are refined through controlled grinding. Surface finishing then removes working marks and brings the blade to the requested practical or presentation finish.

06 06

Fit, Assemble & Inspect

The chosen Kamagong, Mahogany, Chico wood, or horn handle is fitted and finished, followed by its matching scabbard or sheath. Each completed piece is checked for fit, finish, alignment, and overall handling before release.

Customer Policies

Shipping & Legal

Shipping Information

Nationwide orders may be shipped through LBC or another suitable courier. International orders may be sent through UPS or an available tracked service. Shipping fees and estimated transit times are calculated separately and confirmed before dispatch.

International customers are responsible for checking local import, ownership, and carry laws. Customs duties, taxes, inspections, delays, or seizures imposed by the destination country are outside Pangasinan Blades' control.

Production Time

Most blades are made to order and enter the workshop queue after the required down payment is confirmed. The estimated lead time depends on the design, materials, customization, current queue, and availability of fittings. A working estimate will be provided with the quotation; handmade production dates are estimates rather than guaranteed deadlines.

Payment Policy

  • Custom and made-to-order work requires a 50% down payment before production begins.
  • Ready-stock items require full payment to reserve and prepare the item for shipment.
  • The remaining custom-order balance, including shipping, must be paid before dispatch.
  • Accepted methods include GCash, bank transfer, PayPal, and Western Union, subject to confirmation.

Returns, Damage & Repairs

Because each blade is handcrafted or made to the customer's specifications, completed sales are final. Returns are accepted only when an item arrives damaged or has a verified manufacturing defect. Contact us promptly with clear photos, packaging photos, and order details before returning any item.

After inspection, a verified defect may be repaired without charge or replaced when an appropriate replacement is available. Damage caused by misuse, neglect, rust, improper sharpening, unauthorized modification, or normal wear is not considered a manufacturing defect.

Orders, Changes & Cancellations

Customers must confirm that their selected specifications and contact details are correct and that the item is lawful at the destination. Changes requested after work begins may affect price and lead time. Down payments become non-refundable once materials are committed or production has started. Pangasinan Blades may decline unsafe, unlawful, or impractical requests.

Privacy Policy

Information submitted through inquiries and orders is used to prepare quotations, confirm specifications, process payments, arrange delivery, and provide customer support. We do not sell customer information. Details may be shared only with payment providers, couriers, or service partners when necessary to complete an order or when required by law.

Ownership Guide

Care Instructions

After Every Use

Wipe away moisture, fingerprints, sap, food residue, and dirt with a clean soft cloth. Dry the blade completely and never leave it wet, especially inside its scabbard or sheath.

Prevent Rust

Most Pangasinan Blades products use 5160 carbon steel, which can develop rust when exposed to moisture. Apply a thin, even coat of mineral oil after cleaning. Use food-safe mineral oil on blades intended for food preparation, and store the blade in a dry environment.

Sharpening

Maintain the existing edge angle with a whetstone or have the blade sharpened by an experienced professional. Avoid high-speed or excessive grinding, which can overheat the edge, remove too much steel, or alter the blade geometry.

Wood & Natural Handles

Occasionally apply a suitable wood oil or wax to Kamagong, Mahogany, or Chico wood components. Wipe away excess product and allow it to cure. Do not soak wooden handles or scabbards, leave them in direct sunlight for long periods, or expose them to extreme heat. Natural horn should also be kept dry and away from prolonged heat.

Storage

Return the blade to its scabbard only when both are completely clean and dry. For long-term storage, keep the blade lightly oiled in a cool, dry location and inspect it periodically. If the environment is humid, consider storing the blade separately from its scabbard with appropriate moisture control.

Responsible Use & Safety

These blades are sharpened tools. Keep them secured and out of reach of children, use appropriate protective practices, and never handle or carry a blade carelessly. The owner is responsible for safe use, storage, transport, and compliance with applicable laws.
