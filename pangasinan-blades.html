Our Heritage

Five Centuries
of Steel and Soul

Long before the Spanish galleons reached our shores, the warriors of Pangasinan were already masters of edged steel. The bolo was not merely a tool — it was the expression of identity, sovereignty, and the unbreakable spirit of a people who refused to be conquered.

The Dela Cruz family has been forging blades in the same barrio for four generations. Our great-grandfather learned from the old masters who remembered a time when every household blade was made by hand. That knowledge passed from father to son, whispered over the anvil, shaped through ten thousand hammer strikes.

Today, we carry that responsibility forward. In an age of machine-stamped steel, we choose the harder path — because we believe that what is forged slowly, with intention and heritage, is the only kind of blade worth passing down.

The Craft

The Art of Forging

Every blade passes through six sacred stages — a ritual unchanged for five centuries. This is not manufacturing. This is mastery.

01 01

The Iron Awakens

Raw steel is chosen not by specification alone — but by touch, sound, and spark. A master can read the grain of the metal before it ever touches fire.

02 02

Into the Fire

Steel is brought to critical temperature in a coke forge. The color tells the story — cherry red, orange, then the bright yellow that signals readiness for the hammer.

03 03

The Shaping

Hammer meets steel. Thousands of controlled strikes over hours — each one deliberate, each one moving metal closer to its destiny. The shape emerges through force and intention.

04 04

The Quench

The critical moment. Heated to exactly the right temperature, the blade plunges into quenching oil. This is the instant steel becomes blade — hardened, locked, committed.

05 05

The Edge

Grinding and beveling — where function becomes art. The edge is worked by hand on natural waterstones, refined to shave-sharp precision across hours of patient geometry.

06 06

The Soul

Handle fitting, final polishing, and the forge mark — the craftsman's stamp of authenticity. This is the last touch. The blade becomes a named, signed, irreplaceable object.

Everything You
Need to Know

Every blade we produce is fully functional — forged, heat-treated, and sharpened to a working edge. They are equally suited for display, collection, or practical use. We do not make wall-hangers.
We primarily work with High-Carbon 1075 and 1080 for their excellent toughness-to-hardness ratio. For Damascus pieces, we layer 1075 with 15N20 nickel steel to achieve the distinctive pattern. Our laminated blades use W2 tool steel for the cutting edge core.
Yes — custom commissions are a significant part of what we do. You can specify blade type, steel, length, handle material, fittings, and even request a cultural inscription or design element. Use the contact form to begin the conversation. Commission timelines range from 6–16 weeks depending on complexity.
Yes, we ship worldwide. International orders are declared as "handcrafted cultural artifacts" and we provide full documentation. However, blade import laws vary significantly by country — we strongly recommend verifying your country's import regulations before ordering. We are not liable for customs seizures.
High-carbon steel requires more care than stainless. After each use, wipe clean and apply a thin coat of food-grade mineral oil or camellia oil. Store in a dry location — the leather sheath is beautiful but should not be used for long-term storage as leather retains moisture. Every blade ships with a care card.
Standard made-to-order pieces take 6–10 weeks from confirmed order to shipment. Complex commissions or Damascus pieces requiring pattern welding can take 10–16 weeks. We photograph the forging process and share updates with you at each stage.
Every blade carries the forge mark of the craftsman who made it — a unique stamp pressed into the heel of the blade before the final polish. You also receive a signed, numbered certificate of authenticity on rice paper, documenting the blade's specifications, steel, and the name of the craftsman.
We accept GCash, Maya, bank transfer (BDO, BPI, UnionBank), PayPal, and Wise for international orders. Commission orders require a 50% deposit to begin forging, with the balance due before shipment. We do not accept credit cards directly at this time.
In-stock blades can be returned within 14 days if unused and in original condition. Custom commissions are non-refundable once forging has begun. If a blade arrives damaged in transit, we will replace or repair it at our cost — document the damage immediately upon receipt and contact us within 48 hours.
All blades ship in our signature presentation — a handmade leather roll tied with waxed cord, inside a black kraft box with the Pangasinan Blades seal. Gift orders can include a handwritten message card on rice paper. A premium wooden display box (narra or mahogany) is available as an add-on.
